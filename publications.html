Hybrid knowledge- and data-driven modelling for robust spike detection and sorting in human C-fiber microneurography
Troglio A, Fiebig A, Maxion A, Kutafina E, Namer B
Sci Rep. 2026 Mar 12;16(1):8975
Data Analysis
Analyzing temporal spike patterns in C-fibers recorded via microneurography is challenging due to the use of a single recording electrode, waveform variability, and high similarity of spike shapes across neurons. We present a computational pipeline combining peak detection and supervised classification for spike sorting to improve the analysis of discharges, identified through activity-dependent conduction velocity changes. In the knowledge-driven step, spike templates are extracted from electrically evoked spikes; in the data-driven steps, three feature sets and machine learning models (One-class SVM, SVM, XGBoost) are evaluated. Compared to Spike2 software, the approach achieved higher F1-scores and reduced false positives.

Explainable Spike Detection Algorithm on C-fiber Microneurography Data using Weighted Hermite Variable Projection Neural Networks
Darabos D, Troglio A, Fiebig A, Maxion A, Kutafina E, Namer B, Bognar G, Kovacs P
Annu Int Conf IEEE Eng Med Biol Soc. 2025;2025:1–7
Data Analysis
This paper presents a neural network solution to detect spike events in microneurography data, employing the Weighted Hermite Variable Projection Neural Network (WH-VPNN) model to capture distinct neurophysiological patterns. Addressing low signal-to-noise ratios and extreme class imbalance, the pipeline integrates a knowledge-augmented model, data balancing techniques, and explainable AI methods. This is the first neural-network-based solution for spike detection in microneurography data.

Supervised spike sorting feasibility of noisy single-electrode extracellular recordings: Systematic study of human C-nociceptors recorded via microneurography
Troglio A, Konradi P, Fiebig A, Pérez Garriga A, Röhrig R, Dunham J, Kutafina E, Namer B
PLoS One. 2025 Sep 26;20(9):e0329537
Data Analysis
Sorting spikes from noisy single-channel in-vivo extracellular recordings is challenging, particularly due to the lack of ground truth data. Leveraging the stable propagation speed of nerve signals to establish ground truth labels, this study evaluates the feasibility of supervised spike sorting on data from two European laboratories. Raw signal features outperformed more complex approaches, though optimal features remained dataset-specific. The authors recommend tailoring lightweight algorithms to individual recordings and assessing sortability before proceeding with non-time-locked spikes.

Harmonizing neuropathic pain research: outcomes of the London consensus meeting on peripheral tissue studies
Villa S, Aasvang EK, Attal N, Baron R, Bourinet E, Calvo M, Finnerup NB, Galosi E, Hockley JRF, Karlsson P, Kemp H, Körner J, Kutafina E, Lampert A, Mørk M, Nochi Z, Price TJ, Rice ASC, Sommer C, Taba P, Themistocleous AC, Treede RD, Truini A, Üçeyler N, Bennett DL, Schmid AB, Denk F
Pain. 2025 May 1;166(5):994–1001
FAIR & Open Data
Neuropathic pain drug development is hampered by incomplete understanding of pathogenesis and a lack of biomarkers, compounded by scarcity of relevant human peripheral tissues. This white paper reports on a consensus meeting attended by 28 neuropathic pain experts held in London in March 2024. Consensus was achieved on minimal recommended phenotyping, harmonised wet laboratory protocols, statistical design, reporting, and data sharing, with standard operating procedures provided as supplementary protocols.

Semi-Automatic Export of Electrophysiological Metadata to NFDI4Health Local Data Hubs: Use Case of Microneurography odML-Tables
Elwes MR, Troglio A, Abedi M, Golebiewski M, Meineke F, Kirsten T, Namer B, Beyan O, Kutafina E
Stud Health Technol Inform. 2024 Aug 30;317:40–48
FAIR & Open Data
The Local Data Hub (LDH) is a platform for FAIR sharing of medical research metadata. After performing a requirements analysis with microneurography researchers, this work proposes a concept-mapping and workflow to transform and upload metadata to a LDH. A semi-automatic upload extension to odMLtables was implemented, enabling odML-to-LDH concept mapping, data anonymization, and creation of custom summaries — a first step towards integrating improved FAIR processes into the research laboratory's daily workflow.

A modelling study to dissect the potential role of voltage-gated ion channels in activity-dependent conduction velocity changes as identified in small fiber neuropathy patients
Maxion A, Kutafina E, Dohrn MF, Sacré P, Lampert A, Tigerholm J, Namer B
Front Comput Neurosci. 2023;17:1265958
Modelling
An in-silico model was used to elucidate molecular causes of CMi dysfunction as observed in microneurography recordings of small fiber neuropathy (SFN) patients. Microneurography data from 97 CMi-fibers from healthy individuals and 34 from SFN patients were analyzed. Using the NEURON environment with a biophysically realistic CMi-fiber model, a grid search assessed the interplay between different ion channels, Na-K-pump, and resting membrane potential. No single mechanism reproduced the SFN phenotype; the best combination involved increased potassium delayed-rectifier conductance, decreased Na-K-pump conductance, and depolarized membrane potential.

Measuring pain and nociception: Through the glasses of a computational scientist. Transdisciplinary overview of methods
Kutafina E, Becker S, Namer B
Front Netw Physiol. 2023;3:1099282
Review
Pain cannot be measured and quantified objectively — as defined by the IASP, it is a sensory and emotional experience. This review surveys methods to assess pain as a percept and nociception as its biological basis in humans, creating a roadmap of modelling options for computational researchers. Methods covered range from electrophysiology and psychophysics to neuroimaging and computational modelling, with a focus on enabling cross-scale integration.

Decoding Neuropathic Pain: Can We Predict Fluctuations of Propagation Speed in Stimulated Peripheral Nerve?
Kutafina E, Troglio A, de Col R, Röhrig R, Rossmanith P, Namer B
Front Comput Neurosci. 2022;16:899584
Modelling
To understand neural encoding of neuropathic pain, evoked and resting activity of peripheral human C-fibers are studied via microneurography. Spike sorting is necessary before spiking patterns can be analyzed, but standard spike-shape-based methods are insufficient due to single-electrode measurements and high noise. This study models changes in propagation speed resulting from the history of fiber activity with polynomial regression, assessing the feasibility of using these models to enhance spike shape-based sorting.

openMNGlab: Data Analysis Framework for Microneurography — A Technical Report
Schlebusch F, Kehrein F, Röhrig R, Namer B, Kutafina E
Stud Health Technol Inform. 2021;283:165–171
Data Analysis
openMNGlab is an open-source software framework for data analysis tailored for the specific needs of microneurography. It loads data from Spike2 and Dapsys — the two major data acquisition solutions — and builds on top of the Neo software for easy extension to common electrophysiological data formats. It provides methods for data visualization, fiber tracking, and a modular feature database to extract features for data analysis and machine learning.

Modeling activity-dependent changes of axonal spike conduction in primary afferent C-nociceptors
Tigerholm J, Petersson ME, Obreja O, Lampert A, Carr R, Schmelz M, Fransén E
J Neurophysiol. 2014 May;111(9):1721–35
Modelling
A comprehensive mathematical model of human cutaneous C-fibers was constructed, reproducing axonal spike propagation at 0.69 m/s commensurate with recordings from human C-nociceptors. Activity-dependent slowing of propagation velocity was adequately simulated; the property most readily associated with it was an increase in intra-axonal sodium concentration affecting the driving potential of sodium currents. The model was validated against ion channel blockade, temperature, and extracellular ion concentration experiments.
