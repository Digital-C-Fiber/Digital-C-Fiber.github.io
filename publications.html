Hybrid knowledge- and data-driven modelling for robust spike detection and sorting in human C-fiber microneurography
Troglio A, Fiebig A, Maxion A, Kutafina E, Namer B
Sci Rep. 2026 Mar 12;16(1):8975
Data Analysis
Microneurography allows recordings of peripheral human C-fibers in vivo. Spike detection and sorting are essential preprocessing steps but remain challenging due to the single-electrode setup, low signal-to-noise ratio, and overlapping spike waveforms. We propose a hybrid approach combining knowledge-driven features derived from the biophysical properties of C-fibers with data-driven machine learning models to improve both spike detection and sorting robustness across diverse recording conditions.

Modeling the Memory of Unmyelinated Axons: Integration of a Data-Driven Approach with Physiological Memory Concept
Maxion A, Turkin A, Namer B, Tigerholm J, Kutafina E
bioRxiv. 2025 Sep 24; doi: 10.1101/2025.09.24.678232
ModellingPreprint
Unmyelinated axons exhibit activity-dependent changes in conduction velocity — a phenomenon termed axonal memory. We present a model integrating a physiological memory framework (linear long-term and non-linear short-term components) with a data-driven approach, enabling efficient prediction of conduction velocity changes from spike train history. The model is calibrated and validated on human microneurography recordings and provides a computationally tractable tool for large-scale simulations of C-fiber populations.

Explainable Spike Detection Algorithm on C-fiber Microneurography Data using Weighted Hermite Variable Projection Neural Networks
Darabos D, Troglio A, Fiebig A, Maxion A, Kutafina E, Namer B, Bognar G, Kovacs P
Annu Int Conf IEEE Eng Med Biol Soc. 2025;2025:1–7
Data Analysis
Spike detection in C-fiber microneurography is hindered by a low signal-to-noise ratio and high variability of spike shapes. We propose an explainable spike detection method using Weighted Hermite Variable Projection Neural Networks (WH-VPNN), which decompose spike waveforms into interpretable Hermite basis functions. The approach achieves competitive detection performance while providing per-spike explainability, facilitating quality control and clinical translation of automated MNG analysis pipelines.

Supervised spike sorting feasibility of noisy single-electrode extracellular recordings: Systematic study of human C-nociceptors recorded via microneurography
Troglio A, Konradi P, Fiebig A, Pérez Garriga A, Röhrig R, Dunham J, Kutafina E, Namer B
PLoS One. 2025 Sep 26;20(9):e0329537
Data Analysis
Microneurography recordings of human C-nociceptors present a severe spike sorting challenge: single-electrode acquisition, low signal-to-noise ratio, and potential multi-unit activity. We systematically evaluate the feasibility of supervised spike sorting on a curated dataset of human C-fiber recordings. We assess multiple feature extraction and classification strategies and identify conditions under which reliable automated sorting is achievable, providing practical guidelines for researchers applying machine learning to MNG data.

Villa S, Aasvang EK, Attal N, Baron R, …, Kutafina E, …, Denk F
Pain. 2025 May 1;166(5):994–1001
FAIR & Open Data
Neuropathic pain research suffers from limited reproducibility and comparability across studies, partly due to heterogeneous methods for peripheral tissue collection and analysis. This article reports outcomes of a consensus meeting held in London, bringing together leading experts to harmonize protocols for peripheral tissue studies in neuropathic pain research. Semi-Automatic Export of Electrophysiological Metadata to NFDI4Health Local Data Hubs: Use Case of Microneurography odML-Tables
Elwes MR, Troglio A, Abedi M, Golebiewski M, Meineke F, Kirsten T, Namer B, Beyan O, Kutafina E
Stud Health Technol Inform. 2024 Aug 30;317:40–48
FAIR & Open Data
FAIR data sharing in electrophysiology requires structured metadata export pipelines. We present a semi-automatic workflow for exporting microneurography metadata, encoded in odML-Tables, to NFDI4Health Local Data Hubs (LDH) based on the FAIRDOM-SEEK platform. The pipeline maps domain-specific odML metadata to the LDH schema and reduces manual curation effort, demonstrated through a real microneurography use case. This contributes a reusable component for FAIR electrophysiology data management within the German health research data infrastructure.

A modelling study to dissect the potential role of voltage-gated ion channels in activity-dependent conduction velocity changes as identified in small fiber neuropathy patients
Maxion A, Lindeé E, Namer B, Tigerholm J, Kutafina E
Front Comput Neurosci. 2023;17:1265958
Modelling
Patients with small fiber neuropathy (SFN) suffer from neuropathic pain caused by changed activation patterns of mechano-insensitive C-fibers (CMi). We analyzed microneurography data from 97 CMi-fibers of healthy individuals and 34 of SFN patients to characterize activity-dependent conduction velocity changes. Using a biophysically realistic NEURON-based CMi-fiber model with Hodgkin-Huxley ion channel dynamics, we performed a grid search optimization over ion channel conductances, Na-K-pump rate, and resting membrane potential. No single mechanism reproduced the SFN phenotype of unchanged activity-dependent slowing combined with faster normalization of conduction velocity. The best-fitting combination involved increased potassium delayed-rectifier conductance, decreased Na-K-pump activity, and depolarized resting membrane potential, suggesting that combinations of molecular mechanisms — rather than a single target — underlie pain-relevant CMi-fiber dysfunction.

FAIR & Open Data
Reproducible microneurography research requires standardized metadata capture. We introduce odML-Tables as a practical metadata standard for microneurography experiments, enabling structured recording of experimental parameters in a spreadsheet-compatible format aligned with the open metadata markup language (odML). Decoding Neuropathic Pain: Can We Predict Fluctuations of Propagation Speed in Stimulated Peripheral Nerve?
Kutafina E, Troglio A, de Col R, Röhrig R, Rossmanith P, Namer B
Front Comput Neurosci. 2022;16:899584
Modelling
To understand neural encoding of neuropathic pain, evoked and resting activity of peripheral human C-fibers are studied via microneurography experiments. Before different spiking patterns can be analyzed, spike sorting is necessary to distinguish the activity of particular fibers of a recorded bundle. Due to single-electrode measurements and high noise contamination, standard methods based on spike shapes are insufficient and need to be enhanced with additional information. Such information can be derived from the activity-dependent slowing of the fiber propagation speed, which in turn can be assessed by introducing continuous “background” 0.125–0.25 Hz electrical stimulation. In this article, we model the changes in the propagation speed resulting from the history of fiber activity with polynomial regression, assessing the feasibility of using the developed models to enhance spike shape-based sorting.

openMNGlab: Data Analysis Framework for Microneurography — A Technical Report
Schlebusch F, Kehrein F, Röhrig R, Namer B, Kutafina E
Stud Health Technol Inform. 2021;283:165–171
Data Analysis
openMNGlab is an open-source software framework for data analysis tailored for the specific needs of microneurography — a type of electrophysiological technique particularly important for research on peripheral neural fibers. Currently, openMNGlab loads data from Spike2 and Dapsys, the two major data acquisition solutions in MNG. By building on top of the Neo software, openMNGlab can be easily extended to handle common electrophysiological data formats. It provides methods for data visualization, fiber tracking, and a modular feature database to extract features for data analysis and machine learning.

Modeling activity-dependent changes of axonal spike conduction in primary afferent C-nociceptors
Tigerholm J, Petersson ME, Obreja O, Lampert A, Carr R, Schmelz M, Fransén E
J Neurophysiol. 2014 May;111(9):1721–35
Modelling
Action potential initiation and conduction along peripheral axons is a dynamic process that displays pronounced activity dependence. In patients with neuropathic pain, differences in the modulation of axonal conduction velocity by activity suggest that this property may provide insight into pathomechanisms. We constructed a comprehensive mathematical model of human cutaneous C-fibers reproducing axonal spike propagation at a velocity of 0.69 m/s commensurate with recordings from human C-nociceptors. Activity-dependent slowing (ADS) of propagation velocity was adequately simulated; the property most readily associated with ADS was an increase in intra-axonal sodium concentration, affecting the driving potential of sodium currents. The model also reproduced post-action potential excitability changes observed in vivo, and was validated against ion channel blockade, temperature, and extracellular ion concentration experiments.
